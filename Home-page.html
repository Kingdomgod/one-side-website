About Kgosi Seatlholo

Kgosi Thabo Milton Seatlholo is the current Chairperson of the National House of Traditional and Khoi-San Leaders, elected on 31 October 2022 for a period of five years.

Traditional Community

Senior Traditional Leader (Kgosi) of Barolong Boo Rapulana Traditional Community in the North West Province. He has a deep passion for rural development and indigenous knowledge systems, ensuring that traditional knowledge passed through generations continues to guide communities in navigating life challenges and enabling socioeconomic livelihoods.

Community Development Focus

Dedicated to addressing service delivery challenges in rural communities while preserving cultural heritage and traditions. His leadership emphasizes the integration of traditional knowledge with modern development approaches.

Recent Recognition

North West Chief Appointed as Chairperson of National House

October 2022 Official Announcement

The chief of Barolong Boo Rapulana in Lotlhakane village, near Mahikeng, Kgosi Thabo Seatlholo has been appointed as the new chairperson of the National House of Traditional and Khoi-San Leaders (NHTKL).

"We are delighted at Seatlholo's appointment as this is a great achievement for the province and the nation at large. We must remember that traditional leaders are custodians of our cultures and traditions." - Premier Bushy Maape, North West Province

Premier Bushy Maape congratulated Kgosi Seatlholo, noting that he is well deserving of this appointment as he has demonstrated dedication and commitment to issues of traditional leadership, culture, and the development of rural communities.

Key Points:

  • Succeeded Her Majesty, Nkosikazi Dorothy Mhla as NHTKL Chairperson
  • Previously served as Deputy Chairperson for seven years
  • Full support from North West Provincial Government
  • Recognition for dedication to traditional leadership and rural development

Elected SANTLDDR Chairperson in Tanzania

December 2024 Continental Session

Kgosi Seatlholo was elected as Chairperson of the Southern African Network of Traditional Leaders in Drug Demand Reduction during the Continental Technical Experts' Consultative Session held in Arusha, Tanzania from December 10-13, 2024.

This appointment reinforces his commitment to addressing drug and substance use challenges across traditional communities in the SADC region.
